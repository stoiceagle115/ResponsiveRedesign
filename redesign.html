    Introduction to Galileo Galilei

    Astronomer, Physicist and Engineer

    Galileo was born in Pisa, Italy on February 15, 1564. His father, Vincenzo Galilei, was a musician. Galileo's mother was Giulia degli Ammannati. Galileo was the first of six (though some people believe seven) children. His family belonged to the nobility but was not rich. In the early 1570's, he and his family moved to Florence.

    Early Education

    In 1581, Galileo began studying at the University of Pisa, where his father hoped he would study medicine. While at the University of Pisa, Galileo began his study of the pendulum while, according to legend, he watched a suspended lamp swing back and forth in the cathedral of Pisa. This would mark the start of his lengthy career into mathematics and physics.

    Explore Other Important Institutions in Galileo's Life:
    Collegio Romano

    Founded by the Jesuit order, the Collegio Romano attracted the best scientists in the Catholic faith, and Jesuit educators as far away as China turned to them for their judgment on scientific matters.

    Accademia dei Lincei

    In 1611, Galileo was inducted into this academy. Their stated goal was "not only to acquire knowledge of things and wisdom, and living together justly and piously, but also peacefully to display them to men, orally and in writing, without any harm."
